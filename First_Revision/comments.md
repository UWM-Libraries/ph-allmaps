Dear all,

Thanks for your patience and apologies for taking a few extra days.

I really enjoyed this lesson. The tool is fantastic and very useful for a variety of uses, from teaching to dissemination. And your lesson is very well structured and clear, and quite easy to follow in spite of the complexity of some of the steps and the broad variety of subjects you address.

My main concern relates to this last thing: the broad set of issues you address in only one lesson, which I fear might be too much for only one read and which might discourage less experienced readers, even though the lesson has a lot of potential to help beginners in working with digital maps.

So I'd like to ask you to consider two possibilities. The first one is cutting the part about Leaflet and only focusing on the command line, presenting this as one among many advances features of the tool. I also feel that this could be justified considering the substantial number of resources that exist on Leaflet already.
 
A second possibility would rewriting this lesson into two successive lessons: an introductory one, focused on the concept and uses of IIIF and on georeferencing, and a second one about the more advanced features of the tool (you could even expand here by adding other existing features). The first one would be a beginner lesson, while the second one would be intermediate level.

This is for me the main question to discuss. 

I have some other observations about the lesson, but they are minor. Just keep them in mind for when you start working on the second version of the lesson before going to peer review:

- [ ]  I'd suggest stating from the very beginning that the lesson will offer a more beginner-friendly first section and a second, more advanced one. Some comments on technical requirements would be useful, because not all readers will be able to explore and understand a JSON file. You have such a warning in p. 124, but this should be said in the first paragraphs.

- [ ] p. 34: please give full name of UWM the first time. 

- [ ] p. 40: please try to avoid contractions ("we’ve")

- [ ] URLs are not always encoded the same way throughout the text (sometimes in a box, sometimes just text).

- [ ] After p. 45, when you mention the IIIF Tools App, I'd suggest you rephrase a bit to make it clear that one has to copy the IIIF Manifesto's filename, and not the long content of the JSON file. (At least I was myself confused for a second there).

- [ ] p. 188: tiny typo, “The result is not **a** new GeoJSON.”

- [ ] p. 219 and p. 2020: period missing at the end of the sentences.

- [ ] I'd suggest that you summarize a bit in the conclusions.

- [ ] I’m not sure that the conclusions are the right place to credit the creators. A footnote could be a better way to do it, or perhaps with a short sentence in the introduction.

Let me know what you think. This is a fantastic lesson and I'm planning to use Allmaps myself for teaching in the future, so I'm looking forward to seeing the text published.

All the best,
Agustín
